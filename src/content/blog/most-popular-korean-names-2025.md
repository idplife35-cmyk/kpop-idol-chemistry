## 2025 Korean Naming Trends

### Trend 1: Two-Syllable Given Names Dominate

The vast majority of popular names in 2025 follow the traditional two-syllable format:
- First syllable: Often carries the main meaning
- Second syllable: Adds nuance or balance

**Examples:**
- 서 (Seo) + 준 (Jun) = Seojun
- 지 (Ji) + 유 (Yu) = Jiyu

### Trend 2: Soft, Gentle Sounds

Modern Korean parents prefer names with gentle, flowing sounds:

**Popular ending syllables:**
- 윤 (Yun) - soft, harmonious
- 아 (A) - elegant, refined
- 은 (Eun) - graceful

**Less common now:**
- Harsh consonant endings
- Overly traditional sounds

### Trend 3: Gender-Neutral Options Rising

Some names are becoming popular for both boys and girls:

| Name | Meaning | Used For |
|------|---------|----------|
| 하늘 (Haneul) | Sky | Both |
| 별 (Byul) | Star | Both |
| 서하 (Seoha) | Auspicious + summer | Both |
| 윤 (Yun) | Soft, harmony | Both |

### Trend 4: Pure Korean Names (고유어 이름)

While Hanja-based names dominate, pure Korean names are gaining popularity:

- **하늘 (Haneul)** - Sky
- **나래 (Narae)** - Wing
- **보라 (Bora)** - Purple
- **아라 (Ara)** - Sea
- **누리 (Nuri)** - World

### Trend 5: English/Western Influenced Names

Thanks to K-Pop's global reach, some idols use English names that influence trends:

- **Felix** (Stray Kids)
- **Ningning** (aespa)
- **Yuna** (ITZY)
