🎭 Korean Naming Traditions

📚 Name Structure

Korean names typically consist of a family name (성) followed by a given name (이름). Most Korean family names are one syllable, while given names are usually two syllables.

🎨 Meaningful Characters

Korean names often use Hanja (Chinese characters) with specific meanings related to nature, virtues, or aspirations. Each character carries deep cultural significance.

👥 Family Connections

In traditional Korean families, siblings often share a common character in their names, creating a sense of unity and belonging within the family.
